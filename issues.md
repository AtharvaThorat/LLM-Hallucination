Section 4: Initial Results
Score: 5/10
This section has the most problems.
Critical issue #1: n=2 patient records. You evaluated on 2 out of 200 patient records. This is acknowledged as a "pilot," but the problem is that you then spend 1.5 pages analyzing these results as if they're meaningful. With n=2, every number you report is anecdotal. A hallucination rate of 0.00 on 2 records means essentially nothing statistically. Fix: You cannot fix the sample size before submission, but you must be more aggressive about disclaiming these results. Add a prominent caveat at the start of Section 4: "All results in this section are from a pilot of 2 patient records (N=24 model-baseline evaluations) and should be interpreted as pipeline validation, not empirical evidence."
Critical issue #2: Gemini 0.00 hallucination rate is not evidence of good performance — it may be evidence of a measurement problem. If Gemini produces 0.00 hallucination across all baselines including full-context (which shouldn't be hallucination-free by design), your hallucination detector might not be working correctly, or the questions are too easy, or the threshold is too lenient. You never consider this. Fix: Add a sentence acknowledging this: "The uniformly zero hallucination rate for Gemini may reflect conservative generation behavior, easy test cases, or limitations of the automated hallucination detector, and will be investigated in the full evaluation."
Critical issue #3: Your proposed method performs worst on almost every metric except hallucination rate. Token F1 drops to 0.14 (Gemini) and 0.44 (GPT). Evidence F1 drops to 0.00 (Gemini). SAR drops to 0.00 (Gemini). You explain this away as "response brevity" and "a deliberate design trade-off," but you never quantify or formally analyze this trade-off. Fix: You need a Pareto-style argument or a precision-recall curve. Show explicitly: "At what evidence threshold does the system achieve acceptable F1 while maintaining low hallucination?" Without this, a skeptical reader concludes your method just produces "Yes" and claims victory.
